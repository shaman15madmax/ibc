What is a Biotope?


A biotope aquarium is a living replica of a natural aquatic habitat. It goes beyond just keeping fish in a tank‚Äîit recreates the look, feel, and balance of a real ecosystem found in the wild. Everything in the setup‚Äîfish, plants, rocks, driftwood, substrate, and even water conditions‚Äîis carefully chosen to reflect one particular location in nature.

The aim is not only aesthetic beauty but also education and conservation awareness. By replicating rivers, streams, ponds, and wetlands, aquarists showcase the diversity of aquatic life and the importance of protecting these fragile ecosystems.

In the Indian Biotope Championship, participants are challenged to present aquariums that:

Faithfully represent a natural habitat ‚Äì with accurate species, layout, and water conditions.
Tell a story of nature ‚Äì showing how life exists in harmony.
Inspire awareness ‚Äì reminding us that conserving these habitats is essential for the survival of countless species.

A biotope is, in essence, a window into the wild‚Äîcrafted inside glass.

Submission Platform

All entries must be submitted through the official contest page at www.uniquedriftwood.in.
Use the entry form available on the Unique Driftwood website for your submission. Complete the form and attach your image and video files. Only online submissions are accepted. Photographic film, recording media, and printed photographs are not permitted.

Open Participation & Age Requirement

The contest is open to participants worldwide, regardless of nationality. Minimum age requirement: 18 years old at the time of submission.

Age requirement

Participants must be at least 18 years old or must have parental consent if under 18.

Entry Limits

Each participant can submit only one picture and one video. The entry must include a front-view photo of the entire aquarium. All entries must be created in the year 2024.

Photo Requirements

Lighting: Photos should be taken in good lighting conditions to accurately represent the aquascape.
Description: Participants must provide a brief description (up to 150 words) of their aquascape, including inspiration, materials used, and relevant details about the driftwood.

Video Submission Rules

File Format: Videos must be in MP4 format and less than 100MB.
Length: Videos should be between 30 seconds and 1 minute.
Content: The video must show a walk toward the aquarium, starting from a distance, and focus solely on the front view of the setup.
Accuracy: The video should accurately display the entire aquarium and aquascape layout.
Editing: Videos must not be edited, filtered, or altered. They should represent the aquascape as seen, without modifications.

Must include verbal declaration in the same video (Today is (date). This tank is created for IBC-2025)

File Specifications

Image File Format: JPEG format, less than 10MB. RAW data is not accepted. Editing: Images must not be edited or filtered. They should represent the aquascape as captured, without alterations such as trimming, reversal, or retouching.

Point Deduction

Points may be deducted for the following reasons: Incomplete or incorrect entry forms. Incorrect photography or video submissions that do not clearly show the entire aquarium from the front side. Layouts created before 2024. Altered images or videos that do not comply with the rules. Failure to meet specified file format or size limits.

Disqualification

Entries may be disqualified if: They violate the Guidelines and Rules or do not meet application requirements. The contest steering committee deems them inappropriate. They do not meet the submission guidelines or are found to be plagiarized. Participants attempt to manipulate votes or engage in unethical practices.

Rights and Usage

By submitting an entry, participants grant the organizers the right to use the photos and videos for promotional purposes, including on the website, social media, and in marketing materials. Participants retain copyright of their submissions but agree to provide high-resolution images and videos upon request.

Contact Information

Ensure that all contact information provided in the submission form is accurate and up-to-date. Winners will be notified via the email provided in the submission form.

Changes and Updates

The organizers reserve the right to amend the contest rules or submission guidelines as necessary.
